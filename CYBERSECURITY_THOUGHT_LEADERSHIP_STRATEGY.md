## Target Audience Personas

### Persona 1: SOC Analyst / Detection Engineer (Junior-Mid)

| Attribute            | Detail                                                                               |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Experience**       | 0-4 years                                                                            |
| **Pain Points**      | Alert fatigue, imposter syndrome, unclear career path, tool overload                 |
| **Goals**            | Get promoted, build detection skills, earn certifications, get recognized            |
| **Content Triggers** | "How to actually get hired as a SOC analyst," "Stop doing this in your SIEM queries" |
| **Platforms**        | LinkedIn (primary), Twitter/X, Reddit r/cybersecurity                                |
| **Engagement Style** | Likes, shares, saves practical content; comments asking questions                    |
| **Buying Signals**   | Course signups, portfolio template downloads, mentorship interest                    |

**Content they share:**

- "I wish I knew this when I started" posts
- Tool cheat sheets (Splunk KQL, YARA rules, Sigma)
- Career milestone celebrations
- Gatekeeping callouts

## TOFU (Top of Funnel) Content

### Purpose

**Awareness.** Maximize reach. Get saved, shared, and commented. Make people follow you.

### Content Types & Templates

#### 1. Hot Takes / Contrarian Opinions

**Template:**

```
Unpopular opinion: [Common belief] is wrong.

Here's why:

1. [Reason with data]
2. [Reason with experience]
3. [Reason with logic]

The reality is [contrarian truth].

[Call to engagement: "Agree or disagree?"]
```

**Post Ideas:**

- "Certifications alone won't get you hired. Here's what will."
- "Your SIEM is probably worse than a well-tuned spreadsheet."
- "Most 'zero days' aren't zero days. They're zero-day-to-you."
- "The cybersecurity skills gap is a hiring gap, not a talent gap."
- "Stop calling everything 'AI-powered.' Your regex isn't AI."
- "Purple teaming is overrated if your blue team can't triage yet."
- "The NIST framework isn't a security program. It's a PowerPoint slide."
- "Most security awareness training is compliance theater."

**Hook Patterns:**

- "I've been in cyber for X years. Here's the lie nobody talks about."
- "I reviewed 100 SOC analyst resumes. 90% had the same mistake."
- "This will get me hate mail, but [take]."

---

#### 2. Myth-Busting / "Did You Know" Facts

**Template:**

```
MYTH: [Common misconception]

TRUTH: [What actually happens]

Here's what I've seen in the real world:

[2-3 examples or data points]

The takeaway: [Actionable insight]
```

**Post Ideas:**

- "MYTH: Hackers use zero-days. TRUTH: 99% of breaches use known vulnerabilities."
- "MYTH: You need a CS degree for cyber. TRUTH: My best analyst was a former paramedic."
- "MYTH: More tools = more security. TRUTH: Tool sprawl is the #1 reason breaches succeed."
- "MYTH: Phishing is a user problem. TRUTH: It's an architecture problem."
- "MYTH: Cyber is all technical. TRUTH: 60% of my job is communication."

---

#### 3. Framework Introductions (Simplified)

**Template:**

```
You've heard of [Framework]. But do you actually understand it?

Here's the [Framework] explained in [simple terms]:

[Visual or numbered breakdown]

How to use it:
- [Application 1]
- [Application 2]
- [Application 3]

Save this. Share it with your team.
```

**Post Ideas:**

- "MITRE ATT&CK in 60 seconds (with a visual you'll actually remember)"
- "The Cyber Kill Chain is dead. Here's what replaced it."
- "Diamond Model of Intrusion Analysis: Why your IR team needs this"
- "OWASP Top 10 for people who don't write code"
- "The NIST CSF 2.0 changes you missed (and why they matter)"
- "D3FEND explained: The defensive counterpart to ATT&CK"

---

#### 4. "X Things" Listicles

**Template:**

```
[X] things every [role] should know about [topic]:

1. [Insight]
2. [Insight]
3. [Insight]
4. [Insight]
5. [Insight]

Which one surprised you? Comment below.
```

**Post Ideas:**

- "7 things every SOC analyst should know about log analysis"
- "5 things I wish I knew before my first incident response"
- "10 free tools every detection engineer should have in their toolkit"
- "3 things that separate junior analysts from senior ones"
- "8 signs your security program is just compliance theater"

---

#### 5. Stat Dumps / Data Hooks

**Template:**

```
[Startling statistic]

Let that sink in.

Here's what it means for you:

[Analysis]

Here's what to do about it:

[Action items]
```

**Post Ideas:**

- "The average time to detect a breach is 194 days. Here's how to cut that to 1."
- "74% of breaches involve a human element. Why are we still blaming users?"
- "Cybercrime will cost $10.5T by 2025. Your company's budget is a rounding error."
- "There are 3.5M unfilled cyber jobs. But 70% of applicants get ghosted. Explain that."

---

#### 6. Viral Hooks That Work

**Hook Formulas:**

| Hook Type                     | Example                                                                  |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Curiosity Gap**             | "The one thing most security teams forget during incident response..."   |
| **Authority + Vulnerability** | "I've led IR for 50+ incidents. I still get nervous every time."         |
| **Contrarian**                | "Your firewall is doing more harm than good."                            |
| **Specificity**               | "At 2:47 AM on a Tuesday, I found the breach. Here's what happened."     |
| **Social Proof**              | "After 500+ comments on my last post, here's what the community said..." |
| **Fear/Uncertainty**          | "If you're not doing this one thing, you're already compromised."        |
| **Transformation**            | "18 months ago I was stocking shelves. Today I'm a SOC lead."            |
| **Challenge**                 | "I'll bet you $100 your SIEM misses this attack pattern."                |

[Technical explanation with diagrams/code]

Step 2: [Action]
Step 3: [Action]

- [Mistake 1]
- [Mistake 2]

- [Tool 1]: [Purpose]
- [Tool 2]: [Purpose]

Full write-up on my blog: [Link]

## Content Calendar Strategy

### Posting Frequency

| Platform      | Frequency  | Best Times            | Content Type                     |
| ------------- | ---------- | --------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| **LinkedIn**  | 4-5x/week  | Tue-Thu 8-10 AM local | Long-form, stories, carousels    |
| **Twitter/X** | 1-3x/day   | 9 AM, 12 PM, 5 PM     | Threads, hot takes, engagement   |
| **Blog**      | 1-2x/month | Anytime (SEO-driven)  | Deep-dives, tutorials            |
| **GitHub**    | Ongoing    | Anytime               | Projects, tools, detection rules |
| **YouTube**   | 1-2x/month | Saturdays             | Walkthroughs, demos              |

### Weekly Content Mix

```
Monday:    TOFU - Hook post (stat, myth-bust, hot take)
Tuesday:   MOFU - Tactical content (how-to, tool tip, detection rule)
Wednesday: TOFU - Engagement post (poll, question, "X things")
Thursday:  MOFU - Deep-dive thread or carousel
Friday:    BOFU - Portfolio showcase, CTA, or success story
Weekend:   Repurpose (turn week's content into blog/video/carousel)
```

### Monthly Theme Rotation

```
Week 1: Detection Engineering / Blue Team
Week 2: Incident Response / Threat Hunting
Week 3: Career Development / Industry Trends
Week 4: Tools & Automation / Open Source
```

### Content Repurposing Framework

```
1 Long-form blog post
  → 5 LinkedIn posts (different angles)
  → 1 Twitter thread (10-15 tweets)
  → 1 Carousel (8-10 slides)
  → 1 Short video (60-90 sec)
  → 5 Quote graphics
  → 1 Email newsletter segment
  → 1 Podcast talking point

Total: 15+ pieces of content from 1 source
```

### Repurposing Ladder

```
Level 1: Text post → Screenshot for Twitter
Level 2: Text post → Carousel with key points
Level 3: Thread → Blog post (expand each tweet)
Level 4: Blog post → Video walkthrough
Level 5: Video → Podcast episode (audio only)
Level 6: All of the above → Newsletter roundup
```

## Thought Leader Positioning

### Strategy 1: Niche Down Aggressively

**Don't be:** "A cybersecurity professional"
**Be:** "The detection engineer who makes Sigma rules that actually work"

| Broad                | Niche                                  |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Cybersecurity expert | Cloud detection engineering specialist |
| Security analyst     | Healthcare SOC analyst                 |
| Penetration tester   | API security tester                    |
| Security engineer    | Kubernetes security architect          |
| Incident responder   | Ransomware negotiation specialist      |

**Niche Selection Matrix:**

| Factor              | Question                                     |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| **Passion**         | What do you spend free time researching?     |
| **Skill**           | What do peers ask you for help with?         |
| **Market**          | What roles are companies struggling to fill? |
| **Differentiation** | What do few people talk about well?          |

---

### Strategy 2: Contrarian Takes (With Evidence)

**Template:**

```
Everyone says [popular opinion].

I used to believe that too. Then [experience/data].

Here's what I found:

[Evidence 1]
[Evidence 2]
[Evidence 3]

The real answer is [contrarian take].

This isn't just my opinion. [Data/source].
```

**Examples:**

- "Everyone says 'assume breach.' But most teams can't even detect the first one."
- "The industry says we need more automation. I say we need better triage first."
- "They tell you to 'shift left.' But if your pipeline is broken, you're just shipping vulnerabilities faster."

**Rules for Contrarian Takes:**

1. Must be backed by data or direct experience
2. Acknowledge the common view respectfully
3. Provide an alternative, not just criticism
4. Be willing to engage with disagreement

### Strategy 6: Community Building

**Tactics:**

- Create a weekly series people anticipate (e.g., "Detection Rule Friday")

- Collaborate with other creators (joint threads, podcasts)
- Amplify others' work generously (tag, share, credit)
- Create templates/tools people use and attribute to you

## Content Creation Workflow

```
1. IDEATE (Sunday)
   - Review trending topics in cyber
   - Check comments/DMs for questions
   - Select 5 post ideas for the week

2. CREATE (Monday)
   - Write all 5 posts
   - Design 1-2 visuals
   - Record 1 short video if applicable

3. SCHEDULE (Tuesday)
   - Queue posts for the week
   - Set up engagement reminders

4. ENGAGE (Daily, 30 min)
   - Reply to every comment
   - Comment on others' posts
   - Share relevant content

5. REPURPOSE (Friday)
   - Turn best post into blog/thread/carousel
   - Archive content ideas for future use

6. REVIEW (Monthly)
   - Analyze top-performing content
   - Identify patterns
   - Adjust strategy
```
