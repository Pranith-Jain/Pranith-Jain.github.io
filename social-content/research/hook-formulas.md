# Hook Formulas — The First Slide Rule

A hook is the single most important element. If the hook fails, nothing else matters.
Below are 8 proven hook patterns, each with cybersecurity examples.

---

## 1. Contrarian / Myth-Bust

> "Everyone says X. They're wrong."

- "MFA won't save you. Here's why."
- "The SOC is dead. Long live the SOC."
- "Stop chasing CVEs. Focus on attack patterns instead."
- "Your SIEM is lying to you about coverage."

**Why it works**: Cognitive dissonance forces the reader to engage to resolve the conflict.

---

## 2. Data Shock

> "N% of [audience] don't know [shocking fact]."

- "80% of breaches use valid credentials. Let that sink in."
- "93% of SOC alerts are false positives. Your team is drowning."
- "Only 12% of security teams can detect lateral movement in real time."
- "The average dwell time is 204 days. What's hiding in your network?"

**Why it works**: Specific numbers create credibility and urgency.

---

## 3. Curiosity Gap

> "There's one thing [successful people] do differently."

- "The one detection rule that stops 60% of attacks."
- "I found a bug in every SIEM. Nobody talks about it."
- "The framework that changed how I think about threats."
- "What top DFIR analysts do in the first 30 minutes."

**Why it works**: The brain wants to close the loop. Must swipe to find out.

---

## 4. Story Opening

> "Last week, I investigated an incident that changed my perspective."

- "At 3 AM on a Friday, our SIEM lit up. What happened next taught me everything."
- "I almost missed the breach. Here's what saved me."
- "A junior analyst found what 3 seniors couldn't. Here's how."
- "I got phished. Here's what I learned."

**Why it works**: Humans are wired for narrative. Personal stories build trust.

---

## 5. List / Number

> "7 things every [role] should know about [topic]."

- "5 DFIR tools I can't live without."
- "10 MITRE ATT&CK techniques you're probably not detecting."
- "3 mistakes every junior SOC analyst makes."
- "8 skills that separate senior engineers from juniors."

**Why it works**: Sets expectations. Scannable. Easy to consume.

---

## 6. How-To / Tutorial

> "How to [achieve desired outcome] in [timeframe]."

- "How to build a detection engineering lab in 30 minutes."
- "How to write a YARA rule that actually works."
- "How to go from SOC analyst to DFIR in 6 months."
- "How to automate 80% of your incident response playbook."

**Why it works**: Promises immediate, actionable value.

---

## 7. Bold Claim / Hot Take

> "Unpopular opinion: [controversial statement]."

- "Unpopular opinion: Alert fatigue is a leadership failure, not a tooling problem."
- "Hot take: Most pentests are theater."
- "I'll say it: CVE databases are broken."
- "The 'assume breach' mindset is lazy thinking."

**Why it works**: Polarizing content drives comments (algorithm fuel).

---

## 8. Question

> "What would you do if [scenario]?"

- "You find C2 traffic at 2 AM. What's your first move?"
- "Your CEO's email is compromised. Who do you call first?"
- "Can you detect this attack? (Most can't.)"
- "What's the one tool you'd keep if you lost everything else?"

**Why it works**: Invites participation. Comments boost reach.

---

## Hook Anti-Patterns (AVOID)

- ❌ "In this post, I will discuss..." (boring, academic)
- ❌ "Hope everyone is having a great day!" (filler)
- ❌ Starting with your name/credentials (nobody cares yet)
- ❌ Generic motivational quotes (cringe in tech)
- ❌ Long paragraphs on the hook slide (wall of text = swipe away)
- ❌ "Agree?" on the hook (weak, try-hard engagement bait)
